# India FY 2026-27 (AY 2027-28) Income Tax — Individual Taxpayer Configuration Reference

> **Document generated:** 5 May 2026 · **Status:** Authoritative — Finance Act 2026 (No. 4 of 2026) received Presidential assent on **30 March 2026** and is in force from **1 April 2026** [Source: Gazette of India, Ministry of Law & Justice, 30-03-2026]. All tax-rate, surcharge, rebate and cess provisions for FY 2026-27 have been notified. A few peripheral items (CBDT operational rules under the Income-tax Act 2025; expanded HRA city list) are flagged "interim — notification pending" where relevant.

---

## TL;DR

- **No change to slabs, rebate or surcharge for FY 2026-27.** Budget 2026 (presented 1 February 2026) and the Finance Act 2026 explicitly retain the FY 2025-26 framework: New Regime slabs (0/5/10/15/20/25/30%), Old Regime slabs (0/5/20/30%), ₹60,000 / ₹12 lakh Section 87A rebate under New Regime, ₹12,500 / ₹5 lakh rebate under Old Regime, ₹75,000 / ₹50,000 standard deductions, 4% cess, surcharge ladder unchanged [Source: indiabudget.gov.in/doc/memo.pdf — Memorandum to Finance Bill 2026, Para A "Rates of tax for FY 2026-27"; indiabudget.gov.in/doc/Finance_Bill.pdf, First Schedule Part I-A and Part III].
- **Two parallel statutes apply to FY 2026-27.** The Income-tax Act 1961 governs AY 2026-27 (income earned 1-Apr-2025 → 31-Mar-2026). The new **Income-tax Act 2025** (Act 30 of 2025), operative from 1 April 2026, governs **Tax Year 2026-27** (income earned 1-Apr-2026 → 31-Mar-2027), which corresponds to AY 2027-28 — the year your calculator is configuring. Section numbers change (115BAC → Sec 202; 80C → Sec 123; 80D → Sec 126; 80TTB → Sec 153; 24(b) → Sec 22) but **monetary limits, rates and caps are unchanged** [Source: incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/all-topics/e-filing-services/objective-and-scope-new-act].
- **Default regime remains the New Regime (Sec 115BAC / new Sec 202).** Old Regime continues to be optional and is opted into via Form 10-IEA (mandatory only for taxpayers with business/profession income; salaried individuals choose at ITR filing). The Old Regime has not been sunsetted by Finance Act 2026 [Source: indiabudget.gov.in/doc/Finance_Bill.pdf — clause 3 (Income-tax under Act 30 of 2025); incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/new-tax-vs-old-tax-regime-faqs].

### Slab schedule

| 24,00,001 | ∞ | 30% |

[Source: Finance Bill 2026 First Schedule Part I-A Para III (income-tax under Act 1961) and Part I-B Para III (income-tax under Act 2025 Sec 202); indiabudget.gov.in/doc/Finance_Bill.pdf]

### Standard deduction
**₹75,000** for salaried individuals and pensioners [Source: Sec 16(ia) of Act 1961 / Sec 19 of Act 2025 — no amendment by Finance Act 2026; cleartax.in/s/section-115bac-features-new-tax-regime-benefits].

### Section 87A rebate
- **Threshold:** Total income (after deductions) ≤ ₹12,00,000
- **Maximum rebate:** ₹60,000 OR tax payable (before cess), whichever is lower
- **Marginal relief** applies for income marginally above ₹12,00,000 (see Section 10)
- [Source: Sec 87A first proviso (Act 1961) / Sec 156 (Act 2025) — unchanged by FA 2026; bajajfinserv.in/insurance/rebate-under-section-87a]

### 87A on special-rate income
**Section 87A rebate is NOT available against tax computed on special-rate incomes** — namely:
- Short-term capital gains under Sec 111A (equity)
- Long-term capital gains under Sec 112A (equity)
- LTCG under Sec 112 (other)
- Lottery / online-gaming income (Sec 115BB / 115BBJ)

This restriction was inserted by **Finance (No. 2) Act 2024 / Finance Act 2025** as a proviso to Sec 87A, applicable from FY 2025-26 onwards, and is carried forward into Sec 156 of Act 2025. The Income-tax Department clarified this on Budget Day 2026 [Source: upstox.com/news/personal-finance/tax/income-tax-slab-rate-2026-27 — "the ₹60,000 rebate in New Tax Regime under Section 87A of the Income-tax Act, 1961 is not available on incomes for which special rates have been provided in the Act"; ClearTax Sec 87A guide].

> Practical rule for the calculator: Compute tax on slab-rate income separately; apply 87A rebate to that portion only. Tax on STCG-111A / LTCG-112A / 112 / 115BB stands without rebate offset.

### Cess
**4% Health & Education Cess** on (income-tax + surcharge), levied after rebate [Source: Finance Bill 2026 cl. 2(6) and cl. 3 — "shall be further increased by an additional surcharge…calculated at the rate of four per cent"].

### Default regime — confirmed
The New Regime under Sec 115BAC(1A) / Sec 202 is the **default** regime for individuals, HUFs, AOPs, BOIs, AJPs from AY 2024-25 onwards; carried forward into Act 2025 [Source: Sec 115BAC(6) of Act 1961 / Sec 202 of Act 2025; cleartax.in/s/section-115bac-features-new-tax-regime-benefits].

### Opt-out mechanism
- **Salaried/pension/no-business taxpayers:** May choose Old Regime simply by ticking the option in the ITR; no Form 10-IEA required. Choice may be exercised every year.
- **Taxpayers with business/profession income:** Must file **Form 10-IEA** before the ITR due date u/s 139(1) to opt for Old Regime. They get only one switch back (i.e., once they revert, they cannot opt out of New Regime again unless they cease to have business income).
- [Source: incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/new-tax-vs-old-tax-regime-faqs; cleartax.in/s/section-115bac-features-new-tax-regime-benefits — "Should I file Form 10-IEA before filing ITR if I have to opt for old regime? Only if you have business income, you are mandatorily required to file Form 10-IEA"]

## 5. Surcharge — FY 2026-27 (Both Regimes)

### Bracket-by-bracket table

| Income range (₹) | Surcharge (Old Regime) | Surcharge (New Regime, Sec 115BAC(1A) / Sec 202) |
|---|---:|---:|
| up to 50,00,000 | 0% | 0% |
| 50,00,001 – 1,00,00,000 | 10% | 10% |
| 1,00,00,001 – 2,00,00,000 | 15% | 15% |
| 2,00,00,001 – 5,00,00,000 | 25% | **25%** |
| above 5,00,00,000 | **37%** | **25% (capped)** |

[Source: Finance Bill 2026 First Schedule Part I-A Para F (Old) and cl. 2(4)(b) Table Sl.No.10 (New); indiabudget.gov.in/doc/Finance_Bill.pdf — verbatim reproduction of FY 2025-26 rates]

### Maximum surcharge
- **New Regime: 25%** (the 37% rate does NOT apply to assessees taxed under Sec 115BAC(1A) / Sec 202) — confirmed unchanged from FY 2025-26 [Source: Finance Bill 2026 cl. 2(4)(b) Table Sl.No.10 cl. (iii); jurishour.in/notification/finance-act-2026-revised-income-thresholds-surcharge-structure/].
- **Old Regime: 37%** — confirmed unchanged [Source: Finance Bill 2026 First Schedule Part I-A Para F].

### Cap on surcharge for dividend income / capital gains (Sec 111A / 112 / 112A)
**15% cap remains in force** for both regimes. The 25% / 37% enhanced surcharge does **not** apply to the portion of income that is dividend, STCG-111A, LTCG-112, or LTCG-112A. The surcharge on this slice is capped at 15% even if total income exceeds ₹2 Cr [Source: Finance Bill 2026 cl. 2(4)(b) Table Sl.No.1 cl. (vi) (Old Regime) and Sl.No.10 cl. (v) (New Regime) — "the rate of surcharge on the income-tax calculated on that part of income shall not exceed fifteen per cent."; bajajfinserv.in/investments/income-tax-slabs corroboration].

### Marginal relief at surcharge thresholds — formula UNCHANGED

For thresholds **₹50L, ₹1Cr, ₹2Cr, ₹5Cr** (Old Regime) and **₹50L, ₹1Cr, ₹2Cr** (New Regime), the formula in Finance Bill 2026 cl. 2(5) and cl. 3(5) is:

> **Total payable (tax + surcharge) ≤ R + S**, where  
> R = tax + applicable surcharge on the threshold amount (e.g., ₹50,00,000)  
> S = (Total income − Threshold)

i.e., the additional (tax + surcharge) above the threshold cannot exceed the additional income above the threshold. This is the **identical formula** used in the calculator's existing logic for FY 2025-26. **No change** [Source: Finance Bill 2026 cl. 2(5) Table — same threshold pairs (₹50L→₹1Cr, ₹1Cr→₹2Cr, ₹2Cr→₹5Cr, >₹5Cr); memo.pdf "Marginal relief is provided in cases of surcharge"].

> **Confirmation for downstream coder:** The marginal-relief logic at ₹50L, ₹1Cr, ₹2Cr, ₹5Cr surcharge thresholds is **left untouched by Budget 2026**. Existing calculator implementation is correct without modification.

## 9. Worked Examples (FY 2026-27)

> Computations to ±₹1. Cess = 4% on (tax after rebate + surcharge).

### Example A — Salaried, ₹10,00,000 gross, no investments

**New Regime (default):**
- Gross salary: ₹10,00,000
- Less: Standard deduction (₹75,000)
- **Taxable income: ₹9,25,000**
- Slab tax: 0 (₹0–4L) + 5% × ₹4,00,000 (₹4L–8L) + 10% × ₹1,25,000 (₹8L–9.25L) = ₹20,000 + ₹12,500 = **₹32,500**
- 87A rebate (taxable ≤ ₹12L): rebate = min(₹60,000, ₹32,500) = **₹32,500**
- Tax after rebate: **₹0**
- Surcharge: 0 · Cess: 4% × 0 = **₹0**
- **Total tax payable (New Regime): ₹0**

**Old Regime:**
- Gross: ₹10,00,000 less Std deduction ₹50,000 = **Taxable: ₹9,50,000**

- Surcharge: 0
- Cess: 4% × ₹1,02,500 = ₹4,100
- **Total tax payable (Old Regime): ₹1,06,600**

**Better choice: New Regime.** Saving ≈ ₹1,06,600.

## 10. Edge Cases & Gotchas

### Marginal relief at ₹12,00,000 threshold (New Regime) — exact mechanics

**Formula (from Sec 87A second proviso, unchanged by FA 2026):**

```
tax_payable = min( slab_tax, taxable_income − 12,00,000 )
```

Applied only when `taxable_income > 12,00,000` and slab tax exceeds the income excess. Once slab tax ≤ income excess, marginal relief naturally stops applying (you simply pay slab tax).

**Exact crossover point (where relief reaches zero):**

For taxable income X in the band (₹12,00,000, ₹16,00,000]:
- slab_tax(X) = 20,000 + 40,000 + 0.15 × (X − 12,00,000) = 60,000 + 0.15·(X − 12L)
- Crossover: 60,000 + 0.15·(X−12L) = X − 12L
- Let Y = X − 12,00,000. Then 60,000 = 0.85·Y → **Y = ₹70,588.235…**, i.e.
- **X (crossover) = ₹12,70,588.24** (round up to ₹12,70,589 for safe inclusion)

**Above ₹12,70,588.24, no marginal relief is needed**: slab tax (₹70,588.24+) is already lower than (X − 12L), so taxpayer pays full slab tax.

> Note: Many secondary sources cite "up to ₹12,75,000" — that figure is the *salaried* upper bound after adding back the ₹75,000 standard deduction (gross ₹12,75,000 → taxable ₹12,00,000 → zero tax). The pure-taxable-income relief band ends at ₹12,70,588.24 [Source: derived from Sec 87A formula; corroborated qualitatively by ujjivansfb.bank.in marginal-relief explainer].

**Worked example inside relief band — taxable income ₹12,40,000:**

- Slab tax = 60,000 + 0.15 × (12,40,000 − 12,00,000) = 60,000 + 0.15 × 40,000 = ₹60,000 + ₹6,000 = **₹66,000** (unrelieved)
- Income excess over ₹12L = ₹40,000
- 87A rebate is **NOT** available (taxable > ₹12L) — but **marginal relief** caps tax at min(₹66,000, ₹40,000) = **₹40,000**
- Cess: 4% × ₹40,000 = ₹1,600
- **Final tax payable: ₹41,600**

[Source: tax2win.in/guide/section-87a; policybazaar.com/income-tax/marginal-relief-surcharge — example for ₹12.1L gives identical methodology]

### 87A rebate clawback for STCG / LTCG

The Sec 87A proviso inserted by Finance Act 2025 **disallows the rebate against tax computed at the special rates** of Sec 111A (STCG equity 20%), Sec 112 (LTCG other 12.5%), Sec 112A (LTCG equity 12.5% over ₹1.25L exempt), Sec 115BB (lottery 30%), Sec 115BBJ (online gaming 30%). **Continues unchanged in FY 2026-27** [Source: ClearTax Sec 87A guide; carrying forward into Sec 156 of Act 2025 — economictimes/CAalley citing CBDT clarification].

> Rebate **may** still apply against tax on the slab-rate portion of income, even if the taxpayer also has STCG/LTCG, provided slab-rate income ≤ ₹12L (New Regime) / ≤ ₹5L (Old Regime). The capital-gains tax stands separately.

### Cess applicability when 87A reduces tax to zero

**No cess is levied when tax-after-rebate is zero.** Cess (4%) is computed on `(tax + surcharge)` after the 87A rebate. If rebate makes tax zero, 4% × 0 = ₹0. Confirmed [Source: Finance Bill 2026 cl. 2(6) — "the amount of income-tax as specified in sub-sections (1) to (5) **and as increased by the applicable surcharge**…shall be **further** increased by an additional surcharge…calculated at the rate of four per cent. **of such income-tax and surcharge**" — i.e., cess applies to net of rebate].

### Form 10-IEA requirement to opt for Old Regime

- **Salaried / pensioner / no business income:** Form 10-IEA is **NOT required**. Choose Old Regime by ticking the option in ITR-1 / ITR-2. May switch every year.
- **Business / professional income:** Form 10-IEA **MUST be filed before the ITR due date u/s 139(1) (now 31 July or 31 August as applicable)**. Failure → New Regime applies by default. One-shot switch back available; cannot oscillate annually [Source: incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/new-tax-vs-old-tax-regime-faqs; cleartax.in/s/section-115bac-features-new-tax-regime-benefits].

### Default-regime ramifications for non-filers

If an individual fails to file ITR or fails to opt out via Form 10-IEA (for business taxpayers) or fails to tick the Old Regime box (for salaried), **the New Regime under Sec 115BAC(1A) / Sec 202 is automatically applied** — including for purposes of TDS computation by employers (Sec 192) where the employee has not made a regime declaration. Employers have been directed to deduct TDS on salary as per the New Regime by default unless the employee opts out in writing [Source: CBDT Circular C1/2020 dated 13-Apr-2020, continuing relevance; cleartax.in/s/income-tax-slabs].
