Company

What is a Company?

A company is a big business.

Many people own it together.

Each owner owns a small piece called a share (ಷೇರು).

An owner is called a shareholder (ಷೇರುದಾರ).

Example A big bank or a big phone business is a company. Many, many people each own a small share of it.

A Company is its Own "Person"

This is the most important idea. Read it slowly.

In the eyes of the law, a company is like its own person.

It is separate from its owners.

The company can own things and owe money in its own name.

The proper name for this is a "separate legal person". The owners are one thing; the company is another thing.

Your Money is Safe

Because the company is separate, the owners are safe.

Say the company owes money (ಸಾಲ) and cannot pay.

Then the owners lose only the money they put in.

Their home and their own savings stay safe.

The proper name for this is "limited liability" — your responsibility (ಹೊಣೆ) is small and fixed. This is the big difference from a proprietorship, where the owner had to pay from his own money.

Who Runs the Company?

There are too many owners to run the company together.

So the owners choose a few people to run it.

These people are called directors (ನಿರ್ದೇಶಕರು).

The directors run the company for all the owners.

Hard to Start

A company is not easy to start.

You must fill many papers and follow many rules.

You must register (ನೋಂದಣಿ) the company with the government.

Good Things and Hard Things

  • Your own money is safe.
  • Can collect a lot of money from many people.
  • The company lives on, even if an owner leaves.
  • Hard to start — many papers and rules.
  • Must follow many government rules.
  • The profit is shared among many owners.
