Partnership

What is a Partnership?

A partnership is a business that two or more people own together.

Each owner is called a partner (ಪಾಲುದಾರ).

Example Two friends open a tea shop together. Both put in money. Both run it. Both are partners. That is a partnership (ಪಾಲುದಾರಿಕೆ).

They Share Everything

Partners put their money together. This money is called capital (ಬಂಡವಾಳ).

They share the work.

They share the profit (ಲಾಭ).

They also share the loss (ನಷ್ಟ).

They Make an Agreement

Before they start, the partners make an agreement (ಒಪ್ಪಂದ) on paper.

The paper says:

1. How much money each partner puts in.

2. How they will share the profit.

3. Who will do which work.

This paper stops fights later. Its proper name is a "partnership deed".

Partners Must Pay the Debts

Just like a proprietorship, the partners and the business are not separate.

If the business takes a loan (ಸಾಲ) and cannot pay, the partners must pay from their own money.

One more thing: if one partner makes a mistake, the other partners may also have to pay for it.

The proper name for this is "unlimited liability" — there is no wall between the partners and the business.

Good Things and Hard Things

  • More people, so more money and more ideas.
  • You share the work.
  • Easy to start.
  • You must share the profit.
  • Partners can fight if they do not agree.
  • You may have to pay for another partner's mistake.
