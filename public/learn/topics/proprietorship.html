Proprietorship

What is a Proprietorship?

A proprietorship is a small business (ವ್ಯಾಪಾರ).

Only ONE person owns it.

We call that person the owner (ಮಾಲೀಕ).

Example Think of the small shop near your home. One person owns it and runs it. That is a proprietorship.

One Person Does Everything

The owner uses his own money to start the business.

The owner runs the shop every day.

If the shop earns money, the owner keeps it. We call this profit (ಲಾಭ).

If the shop loses money, the owner alone takes the loss (ನಷ್ಟ).

Very Easy to Start

A proprietorship is very easy to start.

You do not need many papers or forms.

You can open your shop quickly.

Some shops need a small licence (ಪರವಾನಗಿ) from the government. But this is easy to get.

You and Your Business are the Same

Here is an important idea. Read it slowly.

In a proprietorship, you and your business are the SAME thing.

Say your shop takes a loan (ಸಾಲ). One day the shop cannot pay it back.

Then YOU must pay the loan from your own money. Even your home savings can be used.

The proper name for this is "unlimited liability". It means there is no wall between you and your business.

Good Things and Hard Things

  • Very easy to start.
  • You are your own boss.
  • You keep all the profit.
  • You alone take the loss.
  • It is hard to get a lot of money.
  • If you stop working, the business stops too.
